Case study 1 — EcoBot-IV: urine-to-power energy autonomy

Autonomous robotic platform harvesting energy from human urine using stacked microbial fuel cells (MFCs).

Problem

Enable reliable autonomous operation under tight energy constraints while concurrently delivering waste treatment functionality.

Approach

  • Scaled and optimised anaerobic MFC reactors and stacking configurations.
  • Designed energy management / harvesting logic to buffer variable generation and prioritise loads.
  • Integrated bioelectrochemical generation with mechatronics to demonstrate self-sustaining behaviour.

Results

  • Demonstrated self-sustainable behaviour with practical energy autonomy constraints.
  • Validated a system-level design approach relevant to energy autonomy and reliability.

Methods & tools

Bioelectrochemical reactor engineering • stacking architectures • energy harvesting/management • system integration

Case study 2 — Underwater bio-electrochemical reactors powering sensors

Small (400 mL) and large (15 L) underwater reactors using complex substrates to power low-energy sensing.

Problem

Extend operating time of low-power underwater sensors through robust, long-duration bioelectrochemical power generation.

Approach

  • Built two reactor scales; inoculated with anaerobic digester sludge.
  • Selected for electro-active and fermentative communities to convert complex substrates into VFAs supporting electricity generation.
  • Performed community profiling (before/after substrate selection) to link substrate choice with microbial functionality.

Results

  • Demonstrated extended-duration powering concept with realistic biological and operational constraints.
  • Generated microbial selection insights informing stability and substrate choice.

Methods & tools

Anaerobic microbiology • community profiling • VFAs analysis • long-duration reactor operation

Case study 4 — Micro-flow BES for real-time biofilm imaging

Micro-flow bio-electrochemical apparatus enabling real-time biofilm observation under confocal microscopy.

Problem

Standard reactor configurations limit high-resolution observation of biofilm kinetics and electrochemical behaviour. A specialised setup was required.

Approach

  • Designed and fabricated a micro-flow BES structure compatible with confocal laser scanning microscopy.
  • Enabled real-time imaging while maintaining electrochemical operation and controlled flow conditions.
  • Used insights to guide design iteration and improve mechanistic understanding.

Results

  • Captured real-time biofilm images supporting kinetics/behaviour interpretation.
  • Created a reusable experimental platform for accelerated learning cycles.

Methods & tools

Device design • microfabrication • electrochemical testing • confocal imaging
